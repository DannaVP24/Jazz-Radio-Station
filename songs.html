Unforgettable Jazz Songs

We bring together in this special compilation the best voices of the genre of all times.

The great voices of jazz have long since disappeared from popular radio, television shows, Hollywood's honeymoon or the excesses of Las Vegas. The echo of Frank Sinatra's voice, the smell of Dean Martin's glass or Ella Fitzgerald's laughing scat have faded forever and year after year, we see the greats pass from the stage to the epitaphs. Meanwhile, we watch as historic jazz festivals program a pantomime of what they once were. They are gone, but their recordings live on.

Lady is a tramp

The Lady Is a Tramp is a song from the musical Babes in arms that was composed by Richard Rodgers around 1937. Frank Sinatra and Ella Fitzgerald made it a hit when they recorded it in 1950.

La vie en Rose

This song has versions of famous musicians such as Louis Armstrong, Grace Jones and Édith Piaf. Its lyrics are of a loving nature, to be exact, about the sensations that a man feels when he embraces a lady. You can appreciate the typical instruments of this style which are: trumpet, piano, double bass, guitar and of course the voice.
