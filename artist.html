Jazz icons

"Jazz is a musical art form that originated in the United States through the confrontation of blacks with European music. The instrumentation, melody and harmony of jazz are derived mainly from the musical tradition of the West. The rhythm, the phrasing and sound production, and the elements of blues harmony are derived from African music and the musical concept of African Americans "

  • Louis Armstrong: Trumpeter, singer and director of an American jazz group.
  • John Coltrane: American jazz saxophonist. He was one of the great figures of be-bop
  • Duke Ellington: American jazz pianist, composer, arranger, and bandleader.

Charlie Parker

The saxophonist and jazz composer Charlie Christopher Parker (1920-1955), born in Kansas City (USA), made more than 60 compositions and more than one hundred records, which led him to be recognized internationally as one of the best players of the alto saxophone.

Ella Fitzgerald

Ella Jane Fitzgerald (1917-1996) was born in Newport (USA). She made her debut at the age of 16 at the Harlem Apollo Theater in New York, she was a member of the band Chick Webb, made dozens of record productions and recorded with artists such as Louis Armstrong.
